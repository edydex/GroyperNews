Terrorist attacks trample Twin Towers on September, 9th. 2001.

Posted on September 11, 2001 by admin

On September 11, 2001, a series of coordinated terrorist attacks were carried out by the extremist group al-Qaeda. Four commercial airplanes were hijacked by terrorists. Two of the planes were flown into the Twin Towers of the World Trade Center in New York City, causing both towers to collapse. A third plane struck the Pentagon in Washington, D.C., while the fourth plane, United Airlines Flight 93, crashed into a field in Pennsylvania after passengers attempted to overcome the hijackers. Nearly 3,000 people were killed, making it one of the deadliest days in U.S. history. The attacks had a profound impact on global security, leading to major changes in U.S. foreign policy and counterterrorism measures.
